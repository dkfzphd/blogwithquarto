In the last few years, artificial intelligence has transitioned from a curious experiment to an everyday workmate. It assists in research, code compilation, and brainstorming sessions. However, the most valuable attribute of AI is not doing the work for us, but rather helping us arrive at a position where we can think deeper, faster.

## 1. Shifting the Focus of Problem Solving

In scientific research, the challenge is rarely just finding the answer, but framing the correct question. When faced with ambiguous research directions, AI serves as an excellent sounding board. By laying out all variables, observations, and constraints, we can instruct the LLM to:
- Separate verified facts from assumptions.
- Identify missing parameters or structural gaps.
- Propose ranked testable hypotheses.

This interactive loop doesn't replace domain expertise; it accelerates clarity.

## 2. Collaborative Coding and Data Science

For biostatistical workflows (primarily in R and Python), AI acts as a peer programmer. Instead of replacing logic, it automates standard boilerplates, formats complex `ggplot2` layouts, and debugs syntax errors. 

> [!NOTE]
> While AI can write functional code, it cannot verify if the statistical model fits the data distribution. The human remains the ultimate check on validity.

## 3. The Future of Human Agency

As automation handles routine tasks, our primary value shifts to curation, ethics, and strategic judgment. The future belongs to those who use machine intelligence as a cognitive lever while retaining deep, independent curiosity.
